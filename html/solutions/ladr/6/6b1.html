Question

Suppose \(e_1, \ldots , e_m\) is a list of vectors in \(\mathbf{V}\) such that,

\[||a_1e_1+\ldots +a_me_m||^2 = |a_1|^2+\ldots + |a_m|^2\]

for all \(a_1,\ldots , a_m \in \mathbf{F}\). Show that \(e_1, \ldots , e_m\) is an orthonormal list.

Solution:

\[||a_1e_1+\ldots +a_me_m||^2\]

\[= < a_1e_1+ \ldots +a_me_m, a_1e_1+ \ldots +a_me_m >\]

\[= < a_1e_1, a_1e_1+ \ldots +a_me_m > + \ldots +< a_me_m, a_1e_1+ \ldots +a_me_m >\]

\[= < a_1e_1, a_1e_1 > + \ldots +< a_1e_1, a_me_m > + \ldots + < a_me_m, a_1e_1 > + \ldots +< a_me_m, a_me_m >\]

\[= a_1\overline a_1 <e_1, e_1 >+ \ldots +a_1\overline a_m <e_1, e_m > + \ldots +a_m\overline a_1 <e_m, e_1 >+ \ldots +a_m\overline a_m <e_m, e_m >\]

Therefore, \(< e_i, e_i >=1\) when \(i=j\) and \(< e_i, e_i >=0\) when \(i \neq j\) for all \(i,j \in \{1,\ldots , m\}\).

Hence, \(e_1, \ldots , e_m\) is an orthonormal list. [QED]
