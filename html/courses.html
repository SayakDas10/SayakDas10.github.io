Courses Taken

Master's in Artificial Intelligence

Semester 1

  • Machine Learning - An in-depth course covering supervised and unsupervised learning techniques, including decision trees, SVMs, and clustering.
  • Mathematical Foundations for AI - Covered linear algebra, calculus, and mathematical proofs, essential for understanding AI algorithms.
  • Introduction to Programming for AI - Basics of Python programming with a focus on applications in AI.

Semester 2

  • Deep Learning - Covered deep neural networks, convolutional neural networks (CNNs), recurrent neural networks (RNNs), and practical implementations using TensorFlow and PyTorch.
  • Natural Language Processing - Focused on text analysis, language models, and sentiment analysis, including applications in chatbots and machine translation.
  • Probability and Statistics - Applied probability theory and statistical methods used for understanding data distributions and inferences.

Semester 3

  • Advanced Machine Learning - Explored advanced topics such as reinforcement learning, transfer learning, and generative adversarial networks (GANs).
  • Mathematical Optimization - Focused on optimization techniques like gradient descent, convex optimization, and their role in machine learning algorithms.
  • Research Methodology in AI - Methods for conducting AI research, including paper reviews, designing experiments, and presenting findings.

Semester 4

  • AI Ethics and Policy - Discussed ethical concerns, fairness, accountability, and transparency in AI systems, including societal impacts and legal considerations.
  • Computer Vision - Covered techniques for image processing, object detection, and practical applications in vision-based systems.
  • Capstone Project - Final project focusing on building an AI model for a real-world problem, using skills acquired from previous courses.
